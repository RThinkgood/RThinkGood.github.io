A Call to Action

Glass, with its transparency, reflects not only our surroundings but also the challenges and opportunities in the recycling realm. As we navigate the complexities of glass recycling, the art of upcycling emerges as a beacon of sustainability. From eco-constructions to artistic expressions, the transformative journey of glass showcases that with creativity and conscious choices, even the most fragile of materials can find a second life, illuminating a path toward a more sustainable future.

Lets Talk About Glass

The Material and Process Intensiveness of Glass: A Transparent Truth

  • Material Composition: Glass, composed primarily of silica, soda ash, and limestone, is a sturdy and infinitely recyclable material.
  • However, the production of new glass demands a substantial amount of raw materials and energy.
  • The extraction of these resources and the high temperatures required for glass melting contribute to the material intensity of glass manufacturing.
  • Despite its recyclability, the road to glass recycling presents challenges. Contamination issues arise when different colors or types of glass are mixed.
  • The need for stringent quality control in recycling facilities adds another layer of complexity, making the process more intricate compared to other materials.

What we do:

  • We are exploring applications for use of glass bottles in construction. The transparent beauty of glass bottles, when embedded in walls, creates stunning and functional structures. From homes to community centers, this method not only repurposes glass but also provides a unique aesthetic.
  • R&D of plastic-glass mixtures for construction and other applications.
  • Repurposing glass bottles into household products like cups, candle holders, and lighting fixtures.
